Unit Convert

How to Use

Convert between units instantly:

  1. Select a category from the pill buttons at the top (Length, Weight, Temperature, etc.).
  2. Enter a value in the From field. The result updates instantly as you type.
  3. Use the dropdowns to change the From and To units.
  4. Click the swap button to flip the From and To units along with their values.
  5. Use the Popular conversions shortcuts at the bottom to quickly jump to common conversions.

Categories

  • Length — Millimeter, Centimeter, Meter, Kilometer, Inch, Foot, Yard, Mile, Nautical Mile, Micrometer
  • Weight — Milligram, Gram, Kilogram, Metric Ton, Ounce, Pound, Stone, US Ton
  • Temperature — Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin
  • Volume — Milliliter, Liter, US Gallon, Quart, Pint, Cup, Fluid Ounce, Tablespoon, Teaspoon, Cubic Meter, Cubic Foot
  • Area — Sq mm, Sq cm, Sq m, Sq km, Sq in, Sq ft, Sq yd, Sq mi, Acre, Hectare
  • Speed — m/s, km/h, mph, Knot, ft/s, Mach
  • Time — Millisecond, Second, Minute, Hour, Day, Week, Month, Year
  • Data — Bit, Byte, KB, MB, GB, TB, PB, KiB, MiB, GiB
  • Energy — Joule, Kilojoule, Calorie, Kilocalorie, Watt-hour, Kilowatt-hour, BTU, Electronvolt
  • Pressure — Pascal, Kilopascal, Bar, Atmosphere, PSI, mmHg

Tips

  • Results update in real time as you type — no need to click a convert button.
  • The formula line below the converter shows the conversion rate for reference.
  • Use the swap button to quickly reverse a conversion.
  • Popular conversions at the bottom let you jump to common unit pairs with one click.
  • Data category includes both decimal (KB, MB, GB) and binary (KiB, MiB, GiB) units.

About

Unit conversion is one of the most common everyday calculations, whether you're cooking, traveling, studying, or working on a project. This tool covers 10 categories with over 80 units to handle virtually any conversion you need.

All conversions are calculated locally in your browser using precise conversion factors. No data is sent to any server. Temperature conversions use exact formulas rather than multiplication factors for perfect accuracy.
